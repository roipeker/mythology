[**Bia**](https://www.greekmythology.com/Other_Gods/Bia/bia.html "Bia") was a Titan goddess in Greek mythology, daughter of the [**Titans**](https://www.greekmythology.com/Titans/titans.html "Titans") [**Pallas**](https://www.greekmythology.com/Titans/Pallas/pallas.html "Pallas") and [**Styx**](https://www.greekmythology.com/Titans/Styx/styx.html "Styx"), and had three siblings; [**Nike**](https://www.greekmythology.com/Other_Gods/Nike/nike.html "Nike"), [**Kratos**](https://www.greekmythology.com/Other_Gods/Kratos/kratos.html "Kratos"), and [**Zelus**](https://www.greekmythology.com/Other_Gods/Zelus/zelus.html "Zelus"). [**Bia**](https://www.greekmythology.com/Other_Gods/Bia/bia.html "Bia") was the personification of force. During the [**Titanomachy**](https://www.greekmythology.com/Myths/The_Myths/Titanomachy/titanomachy.html "Titanomachy"), the war between the [**Titans**](https://www.greekmythology.com/Titans/titans.html "Titans") and the [**Olympians**](https://www.greekmythology.com/Olympians/olympians.html "Olympians") out of which the latter emerged victorious, [**Bia**](https://www.greekmythology.com/Other_Gods/Bia/bia.html "Bia") and her siblings sided with [**Zeus**](https://www.greekmythology.com/Olympians/Zeus/zeus.html "Zeus"), along with [**Styx**](https://www.greekmythology.com/Titans/Styx/styx.html "Styx"). To honour them, [**Zeus**](https://www.greekmythology.com/Olympians/Zeus/zeus.html "Zeus") made the four siblings his constant companions.
